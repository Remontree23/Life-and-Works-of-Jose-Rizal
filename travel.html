Spain (1882-1885)

Rizal traveled to Spain to pursue higher education at the Universidad Central de Madrid. He studied medicine and philosophy, and during this time, he became involved in reformist and intellectual circles advocating for social and political change in the Philippines.

France (1885-1886)

While in Europe, Rizal spent time in Paris, France, continuing his studies and engaging with the intellectual and cultural environment. He also wrote articles and essays on the Philippines' condition, contributing to the reformist movement.

Germany (1886-1887)

Rizal studied ophthalmology at the University of Heidelberg in Germany. His focus on ophthalmology was part of his broader medical education, and he gained practical knowledge in this field.

Belgium (1887)

Rizal visited Belgium during his European travels, he was busy writing his second novel "El Filibusterismo".

Italy (1887)

Rizal visited Italy, appreciating its rich history and cultural heritage. While in Italy, he wrote about his observations and experiences.

United States (1888)

Rizal made a brief stopover in the United States, particularly in San Francisco, on his way back to the Philippines from Europe. This visit was relatively short, and the details are not as well-documented as his European travels.
