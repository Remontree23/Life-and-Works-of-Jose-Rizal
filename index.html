Jose Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

Dr. Jose Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonzo Realonda was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and polymath who played a pivotal role in the Philippines' struggle for independence during the late 19th century. His literary works, including "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," sparked a movement for social reform and inspired the Filipino revolution against Spanish colonial rule. Rizal was also a physician, sculptor, and linguist, leaving a lasting impact on the cultural and political landscape of the Philippines. Learn more

GALLERY OF RIZAL

The Gallery of Rizal stands as a testament to the life and legacy of Jose Rizal, a revered Filipino nationalist and polymath. Housed within its walls are exhibits chronicling Rizal's journey from his formative years to his pivotal role in shaping Philippine history. Visitors are immersed in his literary works, revolutionary ideas, and artistic endeavors, showcasing his multifaceted genius. From rare manuscripts to poignant artifacts, each item unveils a facet of Rizal's profound impact on Filipino identity and independence. The gallery serves not only as a repository of history but also as a source of inspiration, echoing Rizal's call for social justice and national pride.

LUNETA PARK

Rizal Park, widely known as Luneta Park is an iconic park that carries with it a rich history, as it is the location where the country’s National Hero, Dr. Jose Rizal was executed. The park was also formerly known as Bagumbayan during the Spanish Occupation in the Philippines.

CHILDHOOD SWEETHEART

Leonor Rivera-Kipping (née Rivera y Bauzon; 11 April 1867 – 28 August 1893) was the childhood sweetheart, and “lover by correspondence” of Philippine national hero José Rizal. Rivera was the “greatest influence” in preventing Rizal from falling in love with other women while Rizal was traveling outside the Philippines. Rivera's romantic relationship with Rizal lasted for eight years.

ONE PESO

The Philippine one-peso note (₱1) was a denomination of Philippine currency. On its final release, José Rizal was featured on the front side of the bill, while the Declaration of the Philippine Independence was featured on the reverse side.

FAVORITE TEACHER

Rizal admired the Jesuit teacher Francisco de Paula Sanchez for being a model of uprightness, earnestness, and love for the advancement of his students. And through this Jesuit’s seal, Rizal’s knowledge of Spanish advanced significantly in a relatively short time. And from Sanchez, he also learned math, rhetoric, and Greek.

FORT SANTIAGO

Fort Santiago is a 16th-century citadel, a national landmark and a shrine to the hard-won freedom of the Philippines. It’s been a cornerstone of the country’s history, seeing the defeat of Rajah Sulayman the Spaniards, the invasion of the Chinese led by Limahong, and the first raising of the US flag that declared the beginning of American rule.

FAVORITE DISH

According to Narcisa Rizal Lopez, Jose Rizal’s sister, the favorite dish of her brother “Joseng” was Sabaw ng Munggo (Mung Beans Soup) which he garnished with ampalaya leaves. This was a dish the Philippine National Hero liked to serve to family and friends who visited him in Dapitan when he was in exile.
