Novel by José Rizal

Noli Me Tángere (1887)

(literally Latin for 'touch me not', from John 20:17) Noli Me Tangere is a seminal novel by Filipino nationalist José Rizal, exploring themes of colonialism, social injustice, and national identity in the context of Spanish-occupied Philippines.

Novel by José Rizal

El Filibusterismo (1891)

Sequel to Noli Me Tángere El Filibusterismo is a gripping novel written by José Rizal, delving into themes of social injustice and rebellion in colonial Philippines.

Poetry

Mi Último Adiós (1896)

"Mi Último Adiós," penned in 1896 by the Filipino national hero Jose Rizal, stands as a poignant farewell encapsulating his love for his country and his longing for its freedom from Spanish colonial rule.

Poetry

Kundiman

"Kundiman, a traditional Filipino love song genre, carries the essence of unity and inclusivity, as depicted in "Alin Mang Lahi" ("Whate'er the Race"), celebrating the universality of love and humanity."

A La Juventud Filipina

"A La Juventud Filipina" is a patriotic poem written by José Rizal, celebrating the youth of the Philippines and their potential for shaping the nation's future.

"El Canto Del Viajero" by Jose Rizal is a stirring poetic work that encapsulates the wanderlust and introspection of the traveler, reflecting Rizal's profound insights into the human condition and his longing for freedom and justice.
