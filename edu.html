Tertiary Education

During his tertiary education years, Jose Rizal attended the University of Paris (Sorbonne) in Paris, France, starting in the academic year of 1888-1889. At the age of 24, he pursued a dual course of study, focusing on Ophthalmology and Philosophy. Rizal earned his Doctor of Medicine degree in 1888 and followed it with a Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1889, specializing in Ophthalmology.

Trivia

Jose Rizal started learning how to read and write at the age of three. This early education began in 1872 when he was living in Calamba, Laguna. His mother, Teodora Alonzo, served as his first teacher and played a significant role in nurturing his early intellectual development

Primary

Jose Rizal began his primary education at Colegio de San Juan de Letran in Biñan, Laguna, at the age of 9, but he did not complete his degree there. He started his studies in arts but transferred to Ateneo Municipal de Manila due to a conflict with the Dominican friars managing the institution.

The conflict arose when Rizal's older brother, Paciano, was accused of causing trouble among students. To prevent further issues for his family, Rizal opted to continue his education elsewhere, eventually pursuing higher studies in Spain.

Secondary Education

Jose Rizal attended the Ateneo Municipal de Manila from 1872 to 1877, but did not graduate despite pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree. His time at Ateneo significantly influenced his intellectual and moral development, laying the foundation for his future role as a Filipino nationalist and writer.

Rizal's strained relations with some professors and his activism likely contributed to his failure to graduate. Despite this setback, he pursued further education abroad and became a prominent figure in Philippine history.

Tertiary Education

Jose Rizal enrolled at the University of Santo Tomas in Manila in 1877 at the age of 16 to pursue a Bachelor of Arts degree. However, he did not graduate from UST due to conflicts with the Dominican friars who managed the institution. Rizal's progressive ideas clashed with the conservative views of the friars, leading to tensions and ultimately prompting him to transfer to the Ateneo Municipal de Manila.

Tertiary Education

During his tertiary education, José Rizal attended the Universidad Central de Madrid, where he pursued studies in Medicine and Philosophy. He began his academic journey at the university in 1882, and within two years, by 1884, he successfully completed his Bachelor's degrees in both Medicine and Philosophy and Letters. This educational experience at the Universidad Central de Madrid played a significant role in shaping Rizal's intellectual development and future endeavors.

Tertiary Education

During 1886-1887, at the age of 26, José Rizal pursued tertiary education at the University of Heidelberg in Germany. As part of his broader medical education, he focused on ophthalmology during this period. Although he engaged in specialized studies abroad, he ultimately obtained his Doctor of Medicine degree from the Universidad Central de Madrid.

Languages

Rizal was fluent in multiple languages, including Tagalog, Spanish, English, German, and French. He also had a working knowledge of Chinese, Japanese, and Arabic.
